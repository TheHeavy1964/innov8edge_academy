## Designing for Human-AI Collaboration

### The Collaboration Spectrum
Not all tasks are equal. Map your workflows onto this spectrum:

| AI Handles (High Volume, Low Stakes) | Human + AI (Judgment Required) | Human Handles (High Stakes, Unique) |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| First-draft emails | Complex complaint resolution | C-suite negotiation |
| Routine data extraction | Medical triage recommendations | Legal strategy |
| FAQ responses | Financial advice with context | Crisis communication |
| Report summaries | Performance reviews | M&A decisions |

### The Five Keys to Successful AI Adoption

1. **Communication first:** Announce AI before deployment. Explain what it will and won't do.
2. **Win quick:** Start with a task people hate — not a task people love. Automating expense reports feels safe. Automating performance reviews feels threatening.
3. **Train the trainers:** Find the AI enthusiasts on your team and make them internal champions.
4. **Measure and celebrate:** Track time saved, errors reduced, tasks automated. Share wins publicly.
5. **Create the safety valve:** Give people a way to override the AI. Autonomy reduces resistance.

### Defining Your AI Team Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| AI Operations Manager | Adoption, quality, budget, roadmap |
| AI Quality Reviewer | Spot-checks outputs, maintains standards |
| Process Owner | Defines workflows and handoff protocols |
| End User | Executes AI-assisted tasks, provides feedback |
