## The Four Components of AI Governance

### 1. Policy
**What:** Formal written rules for AI use
- Approved tools and platforms
- Prohibited data types (PII, confidential, regulated)
- Required disclosures to customers and regulators
- Acceptable use cases and prohibited applications

### 2. Accountability
**Who is responsible?**
- AI Decision Owner: Human accountable for each AI-assisted decision
- AI Operations Manager: Owns tool quality, compliance, and training
- Legal/Compliance: Reviews AI deployments for regulatory exposure
- Board/Executive: Sets AI risk appetite and ethical boundaries

### 3. Transparency
**What must be disclosed?**
- To customers: That AI is used in decisions affecting them
- To regulators: How AI systems work and what data they process
- Internally: Who has access to what AI capabilities

### 4. Oversight
**How do we maintain control?**
- Regular audits of AI outputs for bias, accuracy, and drift
- Human review mechanisms for high-stakes decisions
- Incident response process for AI failures
- Periodic review of AI policies as regulations evolve
