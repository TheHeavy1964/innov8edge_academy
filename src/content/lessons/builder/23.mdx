# The Intent-Action Gap

This is the **"Why"** of agentic engineering. Why do smart models do dumb things?

## The Problem of Ambiguity
When you give an instruction to a human, they use common sense and shared cultural values to fill in the gaps. 
**AI agents have no common sense.** They are mathematical engines that follow the path of highest probability.

### Bridging the Gap:
1. **Explicit Constraints:** Don't just say "Email customers." Say "Email customers who haven't ordered in 30 days, using a helpful tone, and never mention a discount."
2. **Step-by-Step Verification:** Have the agent present its plan *before* it executes it.
3. **Outcome Monitoring:** Check the results of the actions, not just the code status.

## Intent-Driven Design
Your job as an architect is to narrow the gap until the agent's autonomous path perfectly aligns with your business goals. 

> **Rule of Thumb:** If a human could interpret your instructions in two different ways, the AI definitely will.
