# Deployment: Taking Your AI Live

Building an agent on your laptop is the first step. **Deployment** is how you turn that experiment into a professional tool.

## The Deployment Stack

To go from "Local" to "Live," you need three things:
1. **Compute:** Where the code runs (e.g., Vercel, AWS, or Render).
2. **Persistence:** Where the data lives (e.g., Supabase, MongoDB, or Pinecone).
3. **Connectivity:** How the world talks to it (e.g., APIs, Webhooks, or a Chat UI).

### Cloud vs. Local Hosting
- **Cloud (Standard):** Fast, scalable, and manageable. Perfect for 99% of apps.
- **Local (Privacy):** Running models on your own servers. Essential for sensitive data (Legal, Medical).

### Why "Edge" Matters
Modern platforms like **Vercel** allow you to run AI logic at the "Edge"—servers physically close to your user. This reduces the time it takes for a message to travel, making your agent feel snappy and responsive.

> **Deployment Rule #1:** Never ship without **Logging**. You cannot fix what you cannot see. Use tools like LangSmith or Helicone to audit every single "thought" your agent has in the wild.
