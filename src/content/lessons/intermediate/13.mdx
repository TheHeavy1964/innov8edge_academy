# Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

**Autonomy is a spectrum.** The secret to safe AI deployment is knowing exactly where on that spectrum your task should live.

## The Criticality Scale

### 1. Low Criticality (Autonomous)
- **Impact of Error:** Low/Negligible.
- **Example:** Recommending a product, summarizing an internal Slack thread.
- **Goal:** Full speed, zero intervention.

### 2. Medium Criticality (Supervised)
- **Impact of Error:** Moderate.
- **Example:** Drafting a social media post, filtering leads.
- **Goal:** AI does 90% of the work; human does a 10% spot check.

### 3. High Criticality (Mandatory Checkpoint)
- **Impact of Error:** Severe (Financial, Legal, Safety).
- **Example:** Deleting database records, signing contracts, diagnosing health issues.
- **Goal:** The AI **cannot** proceed without a manual human signature.

## Designing for Trust
By building HITL checkpoints into your workflows, you increase trust across your organization. Stakeholders are much more likely to adopt AI when they know a human is still the final "Source of Accountability."
