# RAG: Giving AI a Library Card

**Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)** is the architecture that bridges the gap between a "smart but forgetful" AI and your massive, ever-changing private data.

## The Three-Step Process

1. **Retrieve:** The system looks at your question and searches your "library" (Vector Database) for the 3-5 most relevant pages.
2. **Augment:** It glues those pages onto your question to create a "Super Prompt."
3. **Generate:** The AI reads the provided pages and answers your question with perfect accuracy and citations.

### Why Vectors?
Traditional search looks for exact words (**Keywords**). If you search for "Salary," you won't find documents about "Compensation."

**Vector Search** looks for **Meanings**. It knows that "Salary" and "Compensation" are the same concept, so it finds both.

### Benefits of RAG:
- **Accuracy:** Source-grounded answers mean fewer hallucinations.
- **Privacy:** Your data stays in your database, not in the AI's training set.
- **Up-to-date:** You can add new info in seconds.

> **Key Rule:** The quality of your RAG system depends entirely on your **Chunking Strategy**—how you break your long documents into smaller, searchable pieces.
