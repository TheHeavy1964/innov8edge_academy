# AI Ethics & Bias

As we give AI more control over our businesses and lives, we must address the **Responsibility** that comes with it.

## The Mirror Effect
AI is a mirror. If we feed it data that contains historical bias, the AI will reinforce that bias. For example, if a hiring AI is trained on resumes from a company that historically only hired one demographic, it will "learn" that those are the only candidates worth hiring.

### Core Ethical Pillars:
1. **Fairness:** Ensuring the system treats all users equally regardless of race, gender, or background.
2. **Transparency:** Knowing *why* an AI made a certain decision.
3. **Accountability:** Who is responsible when an AI makes a mistake? (Hint: It's usually the human in the loop).

## Safety & Accuracy
An AI that is 99% accurate but 100% confident can be dangerous. Ethical AI usage requires **verification**. We must always treat AI as a collaborator, not an absolute source of truth.
