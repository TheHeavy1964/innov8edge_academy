# Security & Privacy

In the age of AI, **Data is the New Perimeter**. Protecting your business requires a shift in how you think about "The Prompt."

## The Public vs. Private Trap
Most free AI tools use your inputs to train their future models. If you paste a secret project plan into a public AI, you are essentially "leaking" that plan to the model's developers and, eventually, its other users.

### Security Best Practices:
1. **Anonymize Data:** Never include names, emails, or financial IDs in a prompt.
2. **Enterprise Silos:** Always use corporate-grade AI accounts that offer "Opt-out of training" clauses.
3. **API First:** Accessing AI via API (like we do in the Academy) is generally more secure as providers have stricter privacy agreements for developers.

## The Human Firewall
Technology can only protect so much. Security in AI starts with the user—understanding that every word you type into a chatbox is potentially being recorded. Treat AI as a public forum unless you are 100% sure you are in a private enterprise environment.
