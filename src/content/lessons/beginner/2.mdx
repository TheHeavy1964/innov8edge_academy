# What is an LLM?

Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini are the "brains" behind the current AI revolution. But how do they actually function?

## The Token Prediction Engine

At its simplest level, an LLM is a **next-token predictor**. 

Imagine I say: *"The cat sat on the..."*
The LLM doesn't "think" about furniture. It calculates that "mat" has an 85% probability, "floor" has a 10% probability, and "moon" has a 0.01% probability.

### Key Mechanics:
1. **Training:** The model reads trillions of words to learn the relationship between them.
2. **Embeddings:** Words are converted into numbers (vectors) so the computer can calculate "distance" between meanings (e.g., 'King' is closer to 'Queen' than to 'Apple').
3. **Attention:** The model learns which words in a sentence are the most important for understanding context.

## It’s All About Math
When you talk to an LLM, you aren't talking to a person. You are interacting with a massive, multi-dimensional map of human knowledge. The "intelligence" emerges from the sheer scale of the patterns it has memorized.
